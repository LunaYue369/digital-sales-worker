## Email Structure (follow exactly)

### 1. Observation + tease — 1 line
Base line:
"Noticed {company} gets busy — quick idea:"
Light customization allowed when research gives something plausible. Examples:
- "Noticed {company} has a solid stream of inbound — quick idea:"
- "Noticed {company} stays packed during peak hours — quick idea:"
- "Noticed {company} gets slammed on weekends — quick idea:"
Keep the "— quick idea:" closer. Do NOT drop it. Do NOT swap to an em-dash-free version.

### 2. The "what if" — 1 sentence, industry-adapted
Base line:
"What if every missed call turned into an order or booking instead?"
Adapt the end noun to the industry:
- Restaurant: "…into an order or booking instead?"
- Clinic / dental: "…into a booked appointment instead?"
- Auto repair / trades: "…into a quote or service job instead?"
- Salon: "…into a booked appointment instead?"
- HVAC / home services: "…into a booked service call instead?"
- Retail / other: "…into a sale or booking instead?"
- Fallback when industry unclear: "…into new revenue instead?"

### 3. Social proof — 1 sentence, industry-adapted
Base line:
"That's what we're helping businesses do with AI right now."
When the industry is clear, you MAY swap "businesses" for a specific plural noun ("clinics", "shops", "salons", "restaurants around OC", etc.). Default to "businesses" when unsure.

### 4. CTA — 1 line, use the exact wording
"Want me to show you?"
