## Email Structure (follow exactly)

### 1. Field-observation hook — 1 sentence, customized per company
Open with a plausible, concrete observation about THEIR specific business, tuned to their industry. This is the part that must feel personal. Examples by industry:
- **Restaurant**: "I was in {company} recently and noticed how busy it gets — especially during peak hours."
- **Dental / clinic**: "Drove past {company} the other day — steady patient traffic, which usually means a packed phone line."
- **Auto repair**: "Saw {company}'s reviews — clearly a go-to shop in the area, so I can only imagine the quote calls stacking up."
- **HVAC / home services**: "Checked out {company} — clearly in demand, especially heading into peak season when the phone never stops."
- **Salon / barbershop**: "Walked past {company} on a Friday — packed chair. That usually means a lot of booking calls coming in."
- **Retail**: "Stopped into {company} the other week — the foot traffic alone tells me the phone rings non-stop too."
Never use a generic opener ("I came across your website"). Always tie to something plausible about their business + industry.

### 2. Pain + solution bridge — 2–3 sentences
Tie the observation to the missed-call pain, mentioning *their type* of customer interaction, then introduce Telalive as the fix:
"A lot of [spots / clinics / shops / businesses] we work with were missing calls during rush hours ({industry-relevant: orders + reservations / booking requests / quote calls / scheduling}), so we built a simple AI system that answers every call, books, and captures missed revenue automatically."
Adapt the noun ("spots" for restaurants, "clinics" for medical, "shops" for trades, "practices" for professional services, "businesses" as the safe default).

### 3. Social proof — 1 sentence
"It's been working well for [spots / clinics / shops / businesses] similar to yours around Orange County."
Use the same industry noun as in step 2.

### 4. CTA — 1 sentence
"Would you be open to a quick 10–15 min walkthrough this week to see if it makes sense for you?"
Keep it soft. End the body here. Code adds the sign-off.
